üí° What is Find-S?

The Find-S algorithm finds the most specific hypothesis that fits all positive examples in a dataset.

üõ†Ô∏è How does it work?

It starts with the most specific hypothesis and generalizes it only when necessary to match positive examples.

üìä How should datasets be prepared?

Datasets should have consistent features, include only positive examples for training, and no missing values.

üß™ What is a hypothesis in Find-S?

It's a representation of the concept being learned, updated through iterations over training data.

üìò Can Find-S handle noisy data?

Not effectively. Find-S assumes that all positive examples are correct, so it's sensitive to noise.

üß† Quiz: What happens if the second training example contradicts the first?

The hypothesis is not updated because Find-S ignores negative examples.
